How We Avoid Chaos
Every experiment must earn its way forward as we do not scale every app.
Typical scale-or-kill gates include:
Retention / repeat usage (are users coming back?)


Activation into the core action (e.g., transactions, creation, referrals)


Revenue signal (where applicable)


Security and fraud thresholds (minimum bar before scale)


Partner distribution signal (does a rail reliably convert?)
If an experiment doesn’t meet signal thresholds, we pivot or stop.

Onchain Ownership
Zeno is designed as a native onchain business and plans to incorporate as a DAO LLC using MIDAO, which provides a practical legal entity wrapper (e.g., contracting, holding assets), and defines membership interests that may be represented by digital assets / governance tokens.

$ZENO Flywheel
We intend to allocate a portion of $ZENO to create permanent liquidity pools pairing $ZENO with tokens from apps (app coins) we build or co-build ($APP) under a fee tier.
Draft mechanism (per $ZENO/$APP pool):
Liquidity Pools (LPs) are tokenholders-owned and intended for long-duration.


Fees paid in $ZENO are burned (supply reduction tied to ecosystem activity).


Fees paid in $APP accrue to the Zeno treasury as assets (a growing portfolio exposure to the ecosystem).
Intended outcome:
$ZENO supply reduction becomes usage-linked (rather than narrative-linked).


The treasury accumulates a diversified basket of $APP tokens through activity, aligning builders, partners, and tokenholders around shared growth.
Expected safeguards (governance-controlled):
eligibility requirements before creating a pool (security review, minimum liquidity, partner alignment)


concentration limits per $APP token


ability to pause/adjust parameters via governance with time delays
This flywheel is designed to be measurable and activity-driven, not a promise of returns.
